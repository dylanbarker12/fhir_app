First, some basic things to know about insulin...


The bolus dose for food coverage is prescribed as an insulin to carbohydrate ratio (I:C). The I:C ratio represents how many grams of carbohydrate are covered or disposed of by 1 unit of insulin.


Generally, 1 unit of rapid-acting insulin will dispose of 12-15 grams of carbohydrate, i.e., this will drop the blood glucose by 50 mg/dl.


This drop in blood sugar can range from 30-100 mg/dl or more, depending on individual insulin sensitivities, and other circumstances.
